About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Sports Engineering Lab (SEL), Seoul National University. I build predictive musculoskeletal digital twins of human movement.

My work sits at the intersection of biomechanics, human motor control, and machine learning. I combine imitation and reinforcement learning with equilibrium-point (referent) control to create simulations that not only reproduce how people walk, but also predict how they respond to external forces — such as slips, perturbations, and wearable-robot assistance — in ways that can be checked against measured kinematics, ground reaction forces, and EMG.

I am currently on the job market. My goal is to bridge physically grounded musculoskeletal modeling and modern learning-based control for rehabilitation, assistive robotics, and human performance.
