About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Sports Engineering Lab (SEL), Seoul National University (expected Ph.D., Feb 2027). My goal is to integrate biomechanics, wearable robotics, and digital human simulation to evaluate, understand, and predict human movement.

My research spans three connected themes: human–robot interaction (how assistive wearables change coordination and muscle demand), human response analysis (slip and perturbation experiments on instrumented treadmills), and human movement prediction through musculoskeletal simulation combined with imitation and reinforcement learning. I develop custom experimental software and predictive models that can be validated against measured kinematics, ground reaction forces, and EMG.

I am currently on the job market and seeking a postdoctoral position. I aim to bridge experimentally grounded biomechanics, musculoskeletal modeling, and learning-based control for rehabilitation, assistive robotics, and human performance.
