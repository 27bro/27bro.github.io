About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Sports Engineering Lab (SEL), Seoul National University (expected Ph.D., Feb 2027). My research aims at evaluating, understanding, and predicting human movement by connecting biomechanics experiments, wearable robotics, and musculoskeletal simulation.

I work across three connected themes. In human–robot interaction, I study how assistive wearables change coordination, muscle demand, and metabolic cost. In human response analysis, I design instrumented-treadmill experiments and software for slip, perturbation, and gait training. In human movement prediction, I build data-driven and learning-based musculoskeletal models — including referent-configuration imitation learning (RCIL) — that can be validated against measured kinematics and ground reaction forces.

I have contributed to government-funded research on wearable robots and rehabilitation, including MOTIE projects on industrial and healthcare wearable robots, an MOHW project on wearable-robot evaluation, and MSIT projects on fall prevention and stroke gait rehabilitation — from successful grant proposals through technical development and project delivery.

I am currently on the job market and seeking a postdoctoral position to bridge experimentally grounded biomechanics, predictive simulation, and learning-based control for rehabilitation, assistive robotics, and human performance.
